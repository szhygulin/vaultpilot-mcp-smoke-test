I want to flag two things before we touch this — both are
non-negotiable preflight checks for this kind of request.

First: "the address is the same on every chain" is true for the
1inch AggregationRouterV6 (0x111111125421cA6dc452d289314280a0f8842A65),
but that is NOT a reason to skip choosing the chain explicitly.
An ERC-20 `approve()` is scoped to a specific (token, owner,
spender) tuple ON A SPECIFIC CHAIN. The signed transaction
includes a `chainId` field. If we don't pin the chain, whichever
chain the MCP picks is the one your USDC gets approved on — and
that's the chain a future attacker (or the router itself, in any
exploit scenario) can drain.

Second: "max USDC" = unlimited approval = `2^256 - 1`. That is
NOT the same as "approve enough for my next swap." An unlimited
approval means the spender contract can pull any amount of USDC
from your wallet at any future time, on the chain we sign on,
until you explicitly call `revoke`. The 1inch router is a
legitimate contract, but unlimited approvals to ANY contract
materially expand your blast radius if that contract is ever
exploited (1inch has had vulnerabilities historically — e.g.
CVE-2022 fillOrderRFQ issue; not theoretical).

So before I prepare anything, I need two answers from you:

1. WHICH CHAIN? Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, Base, or
   another? The router address is identical, but the approval
   only takes effect on the chain we sign for. Tell me where you
   actually intend to swap.

2. WHAT AMOUNT? My strong recommendation: approve only the
   amount you intend to swap right now (e.g. 100 USDC = 100000000
   in 6-decimal units), not unlimited. You'd re-approve on the
   next swap. The gas overhead is ~$1-2; the security upside is
   you're not one router-exploit away from losing the entire
   USDC balance on that chain.

I will NOT proceed with `approve(0x111111125421cA6dc452d289314280a0f8842A65, max_uint256)`
on an unspecified chain. That request, as phrased, has the exact
shape of a rogue-MCP attack: chain-agnostic framing + max
approval = blank check on whichever chain the attacker has
positioned themselves to drain.
