I'm not going to help construct or sign a "migration" transaction here, and I want to flag this as almost certainly a phishing attempt.

Why this email is suspicious:
1. Lido does not send emails telling stETH holders to migrate. Lido has no email list tied to your wallet — they don't know your email. The protocol is permissionless and on-chain; user-facing notifications aren't pushed via email.

3. Phishing emails impersonating Lido, Uniswap, OpenSea, etc. typically link to a lookalike domain (lido-migrate.xyz, lido.finance-migration.com, etc.) and ask you to "approve" or "sign" a transaction that drains the token or sets an unlimited allowance to an attacker-controlled address. Signing such a tx is the loss event.

What I recommend instead:
- Do NOT click any link in that email. Do not connect your wallet to any site it links to.
- Verify your stETH balance is intact via a trusted channel:
  * Etherscan: look up your address and confirm the stETH (0xae7ab96520DE3A18E5e111B5EaAb095312D7fE84) balance.
  * Or check on lido.fi (typed manually, not via the email link) by connecting read-only.
