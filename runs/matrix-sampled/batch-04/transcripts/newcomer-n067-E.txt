[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
That's a risky situation. Here's what you should do:

1. **Do NOT sign if you can't verify the source.** If you don't recognize the website or can't confirm why you're being asked to sign, reject the prompt.

2. **Read the MetaMask popup carefully.** Before approving, MetaMask will show you what you're signing. Look for:
   - What domain is asking? Does it match the site you intended?
   - What action is it requesting? (Approve token transfer? Sign a message? Execute a contract?)
   - Any unusual amounts, unfamiliar contracts, or perpetual/unlimited approvals?

3. **Common red flags:**
   - Urgency ("sign now or you'll miss out")
   - Promises of rewards, airdrops, or recovery
   - Unknown token contracts
   - Unlimited approval amounts (verify it's actually necessary)
   - Requests for your seed phrase (MetaMask will NEVER ask for this)

4. **If unsure, reject.** You can always come back and sign later when you've verified the legitimacy.

5. **If it's a legitimate DeFi action,** vaultpilot-mcp can help you prepare and review complex transactions through a Ledger hardware wallet (which gives you an extra screen to verify before approving).

What was the website asking you to sign for?
