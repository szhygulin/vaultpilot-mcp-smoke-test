[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Good question. In ERC-20 tokens, "approve unlimited" means you're giving a smart contract
permission to spend ANY amount of your tokens without asking again. Here's why it matters:

**What it is:**
When you interact with a DEX (like Uniswap) or lending protocol, you first call `approve(spender, amount)`.
This sets an allowance—the maximum the contract can withdraw from your wallet. Most dApps ask you to
approve a specific amount (like "100 USDC for this swap").

But some dApps and older contracts ask for `uint256.max` (2^256 - 1)—essentially unlimited. Once approved,
that contract can drain every token of that type you ever own, forever, without asking again.

**Why it's dangerous:**
1. **Perpetual risk.** If the dApp's smart contract is compromised or has a bug, the attacker can steal
   all your tokens of that type instantly—not just this transaction.
2. **Hidden compromise.** A hacked website could silently ask your wallet to approve unlimited USDC
   to a malicious address you never see.
3. **No expiration.** Unlike login tokens, these approvals don't expire. You have to manually revoke them.

**What VaultPilot does:**
- When you prepare any approval, the preview and on-device Ledger screen clearly show the amount
  and spender contract address.
- VaultPilot's preflight invariants flag unlimited approvals (uint256.max equivalents) in §11
  (ApprovalClassScan invariant) to make the unlimited decision explicit.
- Best practice: approve only the amount you need for this transaction. If the dApp insists on unlimited,
  understand the risk—it's often UX convenience on their side, not a technical requirement.

**How to manage existing approvals:**
Use `get_token_allowances` to see every spender that has an approval on your wallet, then revoke
(set allowance to 0) for any contract you no longer trust.

Bottom line: Unlimited approvals trade off convenience for security. They're fine for well-audited,
widely-used protocols (Uniswap, Aave, Lido), but risky for new or obscure dApps. When in doubt,
approve just what you need.
