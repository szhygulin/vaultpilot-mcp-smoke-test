[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! Let me explain the difference:

**Isolated Margin** means each position has its own dedicated collateral pool. If you open a 10x long ETH position with isolated margin, you deposit collateral only for that specific trade. If ETH drops and you hit liquidation threshold on that position, only that position is liquidated — your other positions are unaffected. Your maximum loss per trade is limited to the collateral you allocated to it.

**Cross Margin** pools all your collateral across all open positions on a given exchange or account. If you have multiple positions (long BTC, short ETH, long SOL), they all share the same collateral pool. If any position's losses draw the account below maintenance level, ALL positions can be liquidated at once. However, profitable positions can offset losses from underwater positions, potentially saving you from liquidation.

Key differences:
- **Risk isolation**: Isolated = per-trade risk limit; Cross = account-wide risk
- **Liquidation scope**: Isolated = single position; Cross = all positions
- **Capital efficiency**: Cross typically lets you leverage the same collateral across multiple trades
- **Best for**: Isolated good for risk management on directional bets; Cross good for experienced traders doing paired hedges

Most major platforms (Aave, Compound, Lido) actually use a single global pool model that's closer to cross margin for lending, but isolated lending markets are gaining adoption on platforms like Compound V3.
