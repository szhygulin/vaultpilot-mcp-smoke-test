[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
EIP-7702 is an Ethereum Improvement Proposal that introduces a new transaction type (`tx_type 4`) allowing a regular EOA (like your MetaMask wallet) to delegate code execution to a smart contract without permanent account migration. Here's what it means for you:

**Delegation, not conversion**: You keep your MetaMask wallet as-is. During a single transaction, you can temporarily invoke a smart contract's logic (called a "code delegator") as if it were your own code. After the tx settles, you revert to a regular EOA.

**Use cases**: One-off complex operations (batch approvals + swap, abstract-away gas fees for users, enable multi-sig for a single transaction) without setting up a permanent smart account.

**Risk**: A 7702 delegation during a transaction is as dangerous as calling any smart contract — you're temporarily trusting code you don't own. The attack surface is the delegated contract's logic, not a core protocol gap. Standard auditing applies.

**Current state**: EIP-7702 is in development/research. MetaMask doesn't have first-class UI for 7702 yet; wallet support is expected post-Dencun (if/when it activates). It's NOT a breaking change for you — your existing wallet continues working unchanged.
