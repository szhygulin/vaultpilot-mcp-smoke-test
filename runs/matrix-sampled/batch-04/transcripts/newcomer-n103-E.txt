[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
MetaMask displays warnings to protect you from common DeFi risks. You'll see warnings when:

1. **Approving tokens to a contract** — MetaMask alerts you when a dApp requests permission to transfer your tokens. This is a security feature because malicious contracts can be designed to drain approved tokens.

2. **Interacting with unknown contracts** — Warnings appear for unverified or newly-deployed contracts. VaultPilot avoids this entirely by using read-only portfolio queries first and having you review transaction details on your Ledger device before any signing.

3. **High-value transfers** — MetaMask warns on unusually large sends as a sanity check.

4. **Suspicious methods** — If a contract call signature looks unusual, MetaMask flags it.

The warnings exist because smart contracts run arbitrary code, and once you approve a token or sign a transaction, the blockchain executes it immutably. These warnings are your UX checkpoint before that irreversible step.

With VaultPilot + Ledger, you get an *additional* layer: your hardware wallet displays the exact bytes of the transaction on its screen. You can verify the recipient address and amount match what you intended before physically approving on the device. That on-device verification is your final defense against both MetaMask confusion and rogue contracts.
