[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Great question! Here's the key difference:

**Simple message signing** (sign/sign_message):
- Takes a plain UTF-8 message (e.g., "I am Alice, proving I own this address")
- Signs it with your private key using BIP-137 (Bitcoin) or EIP-191 (Ethereum)
- The signature proves you authorized that exact message, nothing more
- Used for: account recovery, proof-of-ownership, Sign-In-with-Ethereum (SIWE)

**Typed-data signing** (sign_typed_data / EIP-712):
- Takes a **structured data object** with explicit field types (domain, message schema)
- Instead of signing plain text, signs a cryptographically hashed representation
- Device shows the decoded fields on-screen (spender address, amount, deadline)
- Used for: ERC-20 Permit approvals, Permit2 batches, CowSwap orders, advanced authorization flows
- Much higher stake: a Permit signature grants the spender *perpetual* transfer authority for the lifetime of the deadline

On-device, EIP-712 signing on a Ledger shows you the fields decoded and human-readable—you can verify the spender address, amount, and expiration directly. Simple message signing just shows the message text line-by-line. Both require your physical approval.

If you're signing a financial authorization (approve, permit), use typed-data signing where possible—the structured fields give you better on-screen visibility than raw message text.
