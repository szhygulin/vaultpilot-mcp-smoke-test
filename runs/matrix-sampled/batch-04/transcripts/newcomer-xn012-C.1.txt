[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Shorting is a bet that an asset's price will fall. People short crypto for several reasons:

1. **Cyclical markets**: Crypto is highly volatile and doesn't always go up. Bitcoin and altcoins experience prolonged bear markets (2018, 2022) where holding long would lose money. Shorting captures value during downturns.

2. **Risk hedging**: Traders holding crypto long-term may short correlated assets or use derivatives to hedge portfolio risk during uncertain macro periods.

3. **Profit from volatility**: Some investors profit from price swings in either direction, not just uptrends.

4. **Overvaluation timing**: Shorters may believe an asset is overpriced relative to fundamentals and bet on mean reversion.

5. **Leverage and speculation**: Margin trading and futures markets allow shorters to amplify returns (and losses) on directional bets.

Your assumption that "crypto is always going up long-term" is based on a multi-decade trend (Bitcoin's 2010–2024 trajectory), but that's survivorship bias. Many cryptocurrencies launched in cycles have gone to zero. Even Bitcoin and Ethereum experience multi-year drawdowns. Professional traders short these cycles routinely.
