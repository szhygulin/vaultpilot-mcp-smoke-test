[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Yes, a multisig wallet is a powerful self-custody option, especially for larger holdings. Here's what you should know:

**Benefits of multisig:**
- Distributes signing authority across multiple keys, so a single compromised key doesn't expose your funds
- Protects against lost keys: with 2-of-3, you can lose one key and still access your funds
- Ideal for high-value holdings, organizations, or long-term storage

**Common configurations:**
- 2-of-3: Very popular for individuals (personal + spouse + trusted backup signer, or 3 personal devices)
- 2-of-2: Requires both signers for every transaction (strong but requires coordination)
- 3-of-5: Higher threshold, good for organizations or treasuries

**Tradeoffs:**
- More complex to set up and manage than single-sig
- Every transaction requires multiple signing operations
- Requires coordination if signers are different people
- Recovery is slower if you lose keys

**For Bitcoin specifically**, VaultPilot can help you set up and manage a multisig wallet via a Ledger device. You can create a registered multisig policy that enforces consistent signing rules across all participants.

To get started, you'd pair your Ledger, gather xpubs from all cosigners (via their own Ledger devices or signing setup), register the multisig wallet policy, and coordinate signatures on transactions.
