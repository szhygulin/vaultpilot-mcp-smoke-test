**Leverage risks:**
- You'd owe the loan regardless of crypto performance. If the market drops 50%, you still owe 100% of the principal.
- Liquidation risk: some lending protocols (Aave, Compound) auto-liquidate collateral if your health factor drops below 1.0.
- Interest costs: borrowing costs compound daily. Even if crypto stays flat, interest eats into returns.

**Crypto volatility:**
- Crypto can drop 20–80% in weeks. A leveraged position magnifies both gains AND losses.
- Stablecoins reduce price risk but not smart-contract risk.

**Before you decide, consider:**
1. Do you have an emergency fund (3–6 months expenses) already set aside? Loans should not be your first investment.
2. What's your crypto experience level? Leverage is most dangerous when you don't understand what you're buying.
3. Can you afford the loan payments if crypto drops 50%? If not, don't take the loan.

I can help you read your current portfolio, understand lending-protocol mechanics, or prepare specific transactions once you've decided. But the "should I" call is yours to make with a financial advisor if needed."
