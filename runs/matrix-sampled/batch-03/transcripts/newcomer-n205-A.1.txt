Terra/Luna was a major crypto collapse in May 2022. Here's what happened:

**The Mechanism:**
Luna was the governance token of the Terra blockchain. UST was a "stablecoin" (algorithmic, not collateral-backed) that was supposed to maintain a $1 peg via an arbitrage mechanism: users could burn Luna to mint UST at $1, or burn UST to mint Luna at $1. This arbitrage loop was supposed to keep UST stable.

**The Failure:**
In May 2022, UST lost its peg and dropped to $0.10–$0.90. The arbitrage mechanism broke under stress. Luna's price collapsed from ~$80 to pennies as billions of UST were burned. The entire ecosystem unraveled over days.

**The Cause:**
- Inadequate collateral backing. UST relied entirely on the Luna/arbitrage loop, with no hard reserves.
- Cascading liquidations. As Luna price fell, the collateral ratio collapsed, triggering forced liquidations and death spirals.
- Contagion: major Luna holders (Celsius, 3AC, etc.) faced margin calls, triggering broader market panic.

**The Aftermath:**
Estimated $40B in losses across the ecosystem. It was one of the largest cryptographic collapses in history. Founder Do Kwon fled and faced extradition to the US on criminal charges.

This is why modern stablecoin design emphasizes over-collateralization (USDC, USDT backed by real reserves) or governance structures that don't depend on a single arbitrage loop.
