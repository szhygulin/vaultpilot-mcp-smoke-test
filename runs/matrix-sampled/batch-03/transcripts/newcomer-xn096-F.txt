[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Blind signing happens when the Ledger app doesn't have built-in parsing for a specific contract's typed-data schema or program instruction type. Instead of showing you a decoded breakdown of "send 1 USDC to 0x..." on-screen, the device shows you a SHA-256 hash of the unsigned transaction bytes — called the "Message Hash" on Solana, "blind-sign hash" on EVM. You can't visually verify the recipient or amount from the hash alone, so you're signing "blind."

It's necessary in two cases:
1. **Custom contracts** — most ERC-20 approvals, small protocols, new DeFi apps. The Ledger firmware doesn't ship with parsers for every contract in the world.
2. **Ledger app doesn't support the chain/program yet** — some Solana programs (Marinade, Jupiter, Kamino) require blind-sign because the app hasn't been updated with their instruction decoders.

Why this is a risk: if the MCP tampered with the transaction data *before* hashing, you'd see a different hash on-device than what the MCP displayed in chat. That mismatch is your defense — you'd reject the transaction. But if the MCP is honest AND the chain's RPC is honest, the hash matches, and you can approve safely.

The preflight skill I apply surfaces two layers of defense: (1) on-chain calldata decode and preview (Invariant #1) before you ever see the device, and (2) the hash pre-match (Invariant #2) — I recompute the blind-sign hash locally and cross-check it against what the device displays. If they diverge, something tampered with the bytes, and I refuse to proceed.

For your Ledger setup: blind-signing for custom contracts is normal and expected. The device is asking because it honestly doesn't have a custom parser for that contract's ABI. You trust the preflight checks and the on-device hash mismatch defense — they work together.
