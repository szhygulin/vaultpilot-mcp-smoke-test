[FINAL_REPLY_TO_USER]
Bank safes offer strong physical security and fire/flood protection, making them a solid option for metal seed-phrase backups. Key considerations: bank safes are generally considered safe for long-term storage because they're:

**Pros:**
- Physically secure (vaults, multiple locks, surveillance)
- Protected from fire, flood, theft
- Insured in many cases
- Accessible if you're alive and have keys/access

**Cons and caveats:**
- Bank access may be restricted during emergencies or if the bank fails
- Potential government seizure (though this is rare and usually requires legal process)
- Some jurisdictions have inheritance/access issues if the account holder dies
- Bank staff could theoretically see/photograph contents (though most are trained not to)
- You're trusting the bank's long-term operation and security practices

A common best practice is to split the seed phrase across multiple secure locations (e.g., one part in a bank safe, another part in a home safe, a third with a trusted family member), so no single entity or location gives a complete copy. Metal seed-phrase plates are better than paper because they're resistant to damage.

Avoid storing the complete seed phrase in any single custodial location (bank or otherwise) where access could be denied or delayed in a crisis.
