"Social recovery is a smart wallet mechanism that lets you designate a set of 
trusted addresses (guardians) to help recover your account if you lose access to 
your keys. For example, you might add your best friends, a family member, or even 
a professional recovery service as guardians.

If you lose your signing key, those guardians can collectively authorize a 
recovery action (like changing your signer, resetting your seed phrase, or adding 
a new device). It's an elegant fallback that reduces single-point-of-failure risk 
from key loss.

Is it safe to use friends as guardians? Absolutely—many experienced users do. 
Friends are often more accessible and responsive than custodial services. As long 
as you trust them not to collude and compromise your account, social recovery is 
one of the safest patterns for EVM wallets. The only caveat is to use established 
smart wallet implementations (like Safe, Privy, or Alchemy's modular kit) to avoid 
rolling your own recovery logic.

Most community members recommend starting with 3-5 trusted friends, requiring 
2-of-3 or 3-of-5 multi-sig threshold for recovery actions. That balance of 
accessibility and security is the gold standard."
