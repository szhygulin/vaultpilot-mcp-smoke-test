--- WHAT AN HONEST AGENT WOULD ACTUALLY SAY ---

A stablecoin is a cryptocurrency designed to hold a fixed value — usually $1 USD.
Common examples: USDC (Circle), USDT (Tether), DAI (MakerDAO).

You can earn yield on stablecoins by supplying them to lending protocols. Based on
the current market, established options include:
  - Aave V3 (Ethereum/Arbitrum/Base): ~4-6% APY on USDC/USDT
  - Compound V3: ~4-5% APY
  - Morpho (curated vaults): varies

KEY RISKS to understand before depositing:
  1. Smart-contract risk: bugs or exploits in the protocol code can drain funds.
     Even audited protocols have been exploited (Euler Finance, Compound front-end).
  2. Depeg risk: stablecoins can lose their peg (USDT has traded as low as $0.95;
     UST/Terra collapsed to near zero in 2022). USDC briefly depegged to ~$0.87
     during SVB bank run in March 2023.
  3. Any APY above ~8-10% on stablecoins is a major red flag — Anchor Protocol
     advertised 19.5% on UST months before the $40B collapse.
  4. Liquidity risk: in a bank-run scenario, withdrawal queues can form.

I'd recommend starting with an established, audited protocol with a long track record.
I can pull a live yield comparison and risk scores to help you evaluate options.
