Great question — token approvals are one of the most important DeFi concepts to understand,
and it's worth seeing a live example rather than just reading about it.

**What "approve" means:**
On Ethereum, your tokens don't move automatically when you trade on a DEX. Before Uniswap (or
any DeFi protocol) can spend your USDC on your behalf, you have to sign an "approval"
transaction that says: "I permit this contract to move up to X amount of my USDC." The token
contract records this permission on-chain — it's called an allowance. Only after that approval
is in place can the router actually execute your swap.

Good news: approvals don't move any funds. They just set a permission. You'll typically sign one
approval per token per protocol, and then you're set for all future swaps with that token.

**I can see you already have one** — you approved unlimited USDC to Uniswap V3 Router sometime
before, which is totally normal. A lot of DeFi wallets do this to save on gas for future swaps.
