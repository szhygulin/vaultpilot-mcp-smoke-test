The key differences between NFTs (Non-Fungible Tokens) and standard tokens are:

FUNGIBILITY:
- Tokens (ERC-20, SPL, TRC-20): Fungible. Each unit is interchangeable with any other.
  Example: 1 USDC = 1 USDC. All units are identical.
- NFTs (ERC-721, ERC-1155): Non-fungible. Each token is unique and has distinct identity.
  Example: CryptoPunk #1 ≠ CryptoPunk #2. Ownership of specific asset matters.

UNIQUENESS & METADATA:
- Standard tokens: Only have amount and decimals. No metadata beyond the contract.
- NFTs: Each token carries unique metadata (URI, image, properties, attributes).
  Metadata often stored off-chain (IPFS, JSON) or on-chain.

USE CASES:
- Standard tokens: Currency, payments, DeFi (collateral, yields, swaps).
  Examples: USDC (stablecoin), ETH (native asset), UNI (governance).
- NFTs: Digital ownership, art, collectibles, gaming assets, domain names.
  Examples: Art (Bored Ape YC), gaming (Axie Infinity), music rights.

BLOCKCHAIN REPRESENTATION:
- Tokens: Defined by balance mapping (user → amount).
- NFTs: Defined by ownership mapping (tokenId → owner).

TRANSFERABILITY & DIVISIBILITY:
- Tokens: Freely divisible (transfer 0.5 USDC).
- NFTs: Indivisible (cannot split an NFT; you own it or you don't).
