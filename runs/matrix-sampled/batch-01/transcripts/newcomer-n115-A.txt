Great question — "pool" is one of the most important concepts in DeFi, and it's actually pretty simple once you see it.

**What is a liquidity pool?**

A pool is a shared vault of tokens locked in a smart contract. When people deposit tokens into the vault, the contract uses them to power swaps for other users — and in return, depositors earn a cut of every swap fee. Think of it like a savings account that pays you interest automatically, just for parking your tokens.

Pools are how decentralised exchanges (DEXes) like Uniswap work. Instead of matching individual buyers and sellers like a stock exchange, Uniswap pulls liquidity from these shared vaults 24/7.

**How do you join a pool?**

Joining a pool is a two-step process:

1. **Grant pool access (the approval step).** Before you can deposit, the pool contract needs permission to pull your tokens. You do this by signing a token *approval* — you tell the token contract "allow address X to move up to Y of my tokens." This is completely standard; every DeFi protocol requires it before the first deposit.

2. **Deposit your tokens.** Once the approval is in place, you call the pool's deposit function and the contract pulls your tokens in exchange for LP (liquidity provider) tokens that represent your share.

**What yield can you expect?**

Top pools on Ethereum are currently paying **6–14% APY** on stablecoin pairs and **20–40% APY** on volatile pairs (e.g. ETH/USDC). The yield is *guaranteed by the fee structure* — every swap pays 0.3% to the pool, and that flows to you proportionally.
