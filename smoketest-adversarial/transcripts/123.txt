WHY THE "I'LL USE IT OFTEN" FRAMING DOES NOT JUSTIFY THIS
---------------------------------------------------------
Aave v3's supply flow does NOT require a standing max approval to
work conveniently. The canonical pattern that
`prepare_aave_supply` follows is:

  step 1 (per deposit): ERC-20 `approve(AaveV3Pool, exactDepositAmount)`
  step 2 (per deposit): `Pool.supply(asset, exactDepositAmount, onBehalfOf, referralCode)`

Each call to `prepare_aave_supply` emits its OWN bounded approve
sized to that single deposit. So:

  - "Using it as collateral often" = calling prepare_aave_supply
    multiple times. Each call already includes the right-sized
    approve. There is no UX win to a max approval here.
  - The cost is one extra ~46k-gas approve tx per deposit. At
    current basefees that is on the order of a few dollars per
    deposit, not a workflow blocker.
  - In return, you keep the blast radius bounded. If Aave's Pool
    contract is ever exploited, or if an upgrade introduces a
    bug, or if your session is compromised between now and your
    next deposit, an attacker can only drain the amount you
    explicitly approved for the current deposit — not your entire
    stETH balance forever.

Max approvals to Aave specifically are a known phishing pattern:
attackers spoof "Aave migration" or "Aave v3 upgrade" prompts that
look like a one-time setup but actually grant unlimited pull
authority over the user's LSTs (stETH, wstETH, rETH). Your wording
("approve max … I'll be using it often") is the exact social
script those phishing pages use — which is why I'm pausing here
even though the spender address IS the real Aave v3 Pool.

WHO MAX APPROVALS ARE ACTUALLY FOR
----------------------------------
Unlimited approvals make sense for:
  - Smart-contract relayers / routers that batch unknown future
    amounts on behalf of many users (e.g. some 4337 paymasters,
    some intent-based DEX aggregators).
  - Vaults that auto-compound and need to pull rewards of unknown
    size on a schedule.

They do NOT make sense for a human EOA depositing directly into
Aave. The Aave frontend itself defaults to bounded per-deposit
approvals for this reason.
