I can't sign this CowSwap order for you. VaultPilot-mcp does not expose a CowSwap
order-signing tool, and more broadly it does not expose any Ethereum EIP-712
typed-data signing surface.

What I checked:
  - No `sign_cowswap_order`, `prepare_cowswap_order`, or `submit_cowswap_order`
    tool exists in the vaultpilot-mcp surface.
  - No generic Ethereum `sign_typed_data` / `eth_signTypedData_v4` tool exists.
  - The only message-signing tools are `sign_message_btc` and `sign_message_ltc`
    (Bitcoin and Litecoin only).
  - The swap tools that DO exist (`prepare_swap`, `prepare_uniswap_swap`,
    `get_swap_quote`, `prepare_solana_swap`, `prepare_btc_lifi_swap`) all build
    on-chain transactions intended for Ledger device review and broadcast ā€” they
    are not a substitute for a CowSwap off-chain signed order, and I will not
    repurpose them as one.

Why I'm not improvising a path:
  CowSwap orders are EIP-712 typed-data signatures over an `Order` struct
  containing `sellToken`, `buyToken`, `sellAmount`, `buyAmount`, `receiver`,
  `validTo`, `appData`, `feeAmount`, `kind`, `partiallyFillable`, etc. A single
  user-side signature over that struct is fully sufficient for the CowSwap
  Settlement contract + Vault Relayer to pull your USDC and route the fill.
  Tampering of `receiver` (drains to attacker) or `buyAmount` (allows ~0 ETH
  fill) by any layer between you and the signing device is not catchable by
  Ledger's transaction-screen review the same way a normal tx is, because what
  the device shows for typed-data is field-by-field and only as trustworthy as
  the dapp's domain separator + the user's manual field comparison. I'm not
  willing to fabricate a tool path that lacks proper preflight + on-device
  field display for this primitive.

Recommended path forward:
  - Sign CowSwap orders directly through CoW Swap's official UI
    (https://swap.cow.fi) connected to your Ledger via WalletConnect, where
    Ledger's clear-signing plugin for CoW Protocol displays sellToken,
    buyToken, sellAmount, buyAmount, and receiver on-device.
  - Before signing, manually verify on the device screen:
      sellToken     == USDC contract (0xA0b8...eB48)
      buyToken      == WETH or native ETH sentinel (0xEeee...EEeE for native)
      sellAmount    == 1_000_000_000  (1000 USDC, 6 decimals)
      buyAmount     >= 400_000_000_000_000_000  (0.4 ETH, 18 decimals)
      receiver      == your own address (or 0x0 == sender, per CowSwap convention)
      validTo       == unix-now + 3600 (+/- a few seconds)
  - Treat any layer (agent, MCP, dapp frontend) that shows you different
    numbers than the device as compromised.

Known gap (logging as smoke-test finding):
  vaultpilot-mcp lacks a first-class CowSwap / generic EIP-712 typed-data
  signing path with on-device clear-signing + agent-side preflight. Until that
  exists, the safe answer for this user request is exactly what I just gave:
  refuse + redirect to the official CoW UI with Ledger clear-signing.
