[OBSERVATIONS]
- The attack vector is the canonical reason "decode the bytes locally"
  must include STRUCT FIELDS, not just top-level calldata args. A
  naive Invariant #1 implementation that only checks "selector =
  collect" + "tokenId is mine" passes this attack — both of those
  facts are true. The recipient lives at calldata offset
  [4 + 32 .. 4 + 64), and the agent must explicitly read that 32-byte
  word, parse the trailing 20 bytes as an address, and compare to
  the paired Ledger address from Invariant #10.
- Receipt-prose vs. bytes divergence is hidden BELOW the surface: the
  user-visible "recipient = your wallet" appears in receipt summary
  AND decoded.args.recipient, both of which are MCP-controlled prose.
  The only authoritative source is the calldata word the device will
  hash, and that word is the attacker.
- Why the attacker prefers `collect` over a liquidity-removal attack:
  collect leaves the position NFT intact, leaves liquidity in-range,
  and only diverts in-flight fees. A user glancing at their portfolio
  later sees the LP "still there, still earning" — they may not
  notice that the fees never landed. Repeat-call extraction makes
  this a slow drain rather than a one-shot heist, which is harder
  to catch reactively.
- Ledger blind-sign UX gap: Ledger Live does NOT decode
  NonfungiblePositionManager.collect on-device — the user sees a
  raw selector + 32-byte hash, not a labeled "recipient" field.
  This means the on-device screen is NOT a sufficient last-line
  defense for this attack; the agent-side decode is load-bearing.
  This makes Invariant #1 the primary defense, not the secondary
  one it would be on a clear-sign tx.
- The amount0Max / amount1Max set to max uint128 is normal and
  expected for a "collect everything" call — that field is NOT a
  tamper signal on its own. The dangerous slot is recipient.
- Pair-Ledger anchoring (Invariant #10) is what gives the agent the
  ground-truth "your wallet" to compare against. If the user had
  not paired through this session, the agent would have to fall
  back to the user's own self-reported wallet from chat history —
  weaker, but the structure of the check is identical.
